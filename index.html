Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats are supported?

We support PNG, JPG, WebP and other common image formats. PNG format is recommended for best results.

Do the generated images have watermarks?

Completely free and watermark-free. You are free to use the generated images however you like.

Can I use the images for commercial purposes?

Yes, the generated images can be used for any commercial purpose without attribution, no strings attached.

Are my images saved on the server?

No. All image processing is done locally in your browser using Canvas. Images are never uploaded to any server, ensuring your privacy.

What is a browser mockup generator?

A browser mockup generator places your screenshot inside a browser frame (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge) to create a realistic product showcase image. It's commonly used by designers and makers to present web apps, landing pages, and software demos.

Can I change the background color behind my screenshot?

Yes. You can pick a solid color or a gradient background behind your device frame. There's also a transparent background option if you want to layer the result onto another design.

Do you support dark mode browser frames?

Yes. Both light and dark address bar themes are available for Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge frames. This helps match the aesthetic of your brand or website.

What's the difference between browser frames and device frames?

Browser frames show just the browser chrome — tabs, address bar, window controls — and work best for website screenshots. Device frames (iPhone, iPad, MacBook, iMac) display your screenshot wrapped in a realistic hardware shell and are more suitable for mobile apps and desktop software showcases.

Can I generate a screenshot inside a MacBook frame?

Absolutely. Our MacBook screenshot frame puts your image inside a realistic MacBook Pro 16-inch display, complete with the laptop body and bezel. It's one of our most popular options for product demos.

How do I download the result in HD?

Set the export scale to 2x (HD) or 3x (Ultra HD) before clicking download. Higher scales produce larger images with more detail, perfect for retina displays and print.
